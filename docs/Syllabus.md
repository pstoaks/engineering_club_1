# Future Builders: Introduction to Engineering

**Ages:** 12-18 **Duration:** 5 weeks **Format:** Hands-on project-based learning

## Course Description
This hands-on course introduces students to electrical, mechanical, and software engineering through building a complete IoT environmental monitor. Students will create a device that senses temperature and humidity, displays data using colorful LED patterns, and lives in a custom 3D-printed enclosure.

## Learning Objectives
- Experience the complete engineering design process from requirements to final product
- Understand what engineers do in electrical, mechanical, and software disciplines
- Gain practical skills in circuits, programming, and 3D design
- Develop problem-solving and troubleshooting abilities
- Make informed decisions about pursuing engineering education and careers

## Weekly Schedule
### Week 1: Introduction & Requirements Analysis
Introduction to engineering disciplines • Engineering design process • Project requirements analysis • Component exploration

### Week 2: Mechanical Engineering - Giving It a Skeleton and Skin
Purpose and design of enclosures • Introduction to 3D CAD (Tinkercad) • Custom enclosure design • Preparing designs for 3D printing

### Week 3: Electrical Engineering - Making Connections
Basic circuits and electrical concepts • Reading wiring diagrams • Hands-on circuit wiring • Component integration • Troubleshooting connections

### Week 4: Software Engineering - Bringing It to Life
Introduction to programming concepts • Code structure and organization • Reading sensors and controlling LEDs • Hands-on code modification and experimentation

### Week 5: Putting It All Together
Final assembly • System testing and troubleshooting • Project demonstrations • Career and education pathways • Reflection and next steps

## Materials Provided
Each student receives a complete kit including ESP32 microcontroller, temperature and humidity sensor, addressable LED strip, wiring, and custom 3D-printed enclosure. All software is free and open-source.

## What to Bring
- Curiosity and willingness to experiment
- Notebook for ideas and observations

## Resources
All course materials, code templates, and design files will be provided. Students will leave with a complete working device and knowledge to continue learning independently.
